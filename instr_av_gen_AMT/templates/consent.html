We need your consent to proceed

You have been invited to take part in a research study to learn more about human emotion and cognition. This study is being conducted by Dr Agnes Norbury, Research Associate at the Computational and Biological Learning Laboratory, University of Cambridge.

If you agree to take part in this study, you will be asked to complete the following tasks:

1) View and respond to simple visual stimuli (coloured shapes) on your computer screen

2) Complete some questionnaires related to sensitive topics, including mental health

3) Provide some general information about yourself: specifically your age and gender identity.

There are no known risks associated with your participation in this research. If you become upset while completing the questionnaires, which cover some sensitive topics, you should take one of the following actions: (1) arrange a timely appointment with your physician, clinical psychologist or psychiatrist, or if you are having suicidal thoughts (2) visit a hospital emergency room or urgent care center or (3) please contact the National Suicide Prevention Lifeline (1800-273-8255). Further information and links to mental health resources outside of the US can be found at centreforglobalmentalhealth.org/global-mental-health-websites.

You will receive a $2.50 flat fee for participating, plus you will receive additional compensation depending on the responses you make during the first section of the study - up to around $2.50 extra, depending on how well you do. Participation in this study is voluntary. You may refuse to participate or withdraw at any time without penalty.

If there is anything about the study or your participation that is unclear or that you do not understand, if you have questions, or wish to report a research-related problem, you may contact Dr Agnes Norbury, aen31@cam.ac.uk, Computational and Biological Learning Laboratory, University of Cambridge Dept of Engineering, Trumpington Street, Cambridge CB2 1PZ, UK.

Further information about your rights as a research participant can be found at www.research-integrity.admin.cam.ac.uk/research-ethics.

Any specific information gathered in the study will be disclosed only to the investigator or collaborators. If the results are published, the information remains anonymous and disguised so that no personal identification can be made.

Do you understand and consent to the terms of the experiment explained above?
